---

## Analysis

**Personalization works.** The spear phish achieved significantly higher open rates (+18 pp) and slightly higher click rates (+3.4 pp) than the generic phish. Personalization increases trust — users are more likely to click when the email references their actual manager and department.

**Report rate improved significantly** (+5.5 pp) — likely due to the Wave 1 training being effective. Users are starting to recognize phishing indicators.

**Faster click time** (−1.4 hours) indicates the spear phish was more convincing — users acted on it more quickly without suspicion.

## Repeat Clickers

| Click Pattern | Users | % of Clickers | Action |
|---------------|-------|---------------|--------|
| Clicked Wave 1 only | 12 | 19.7% | Standard training |
| Clicked Wave 2 only | 19 | 31.1% | Standard training |
| **Clicked both waves** | **42** | **68.9%** | **1-on-1 coaching with manager + mandatory advanced training** |

Users who clicked both waves are classified as "repeat clickers" and require escalation.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Finance remains highest risk:** 50% click rate on spear phish — all 6 clickers are repeat offenders
2. **Executives are improving:** 12.5% click rate (down from 25%) with strong report rate — training is effective
3. **68.9% of Wave 1 clickers clicked again** — indicates training alone may not be sufficient for this group
4. **Customer Support is a growing concern:** 40% click rate with PII access
5. **Positive trend:** Report rate doubled to 11.6% — awareness training is paying dividends
